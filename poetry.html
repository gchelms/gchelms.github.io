Sans Expectations: Poetry

"A writer should have the precision of a poet and the imagination of a scientist." ~Vladimir Nabokov

Theory of Relativity

It's a weird feeling waiting for someone to die
We talk about time stopping when you spend time with your lover
That in those moments nothing exists but the two of you
Well, I don't know about you, but for me, time has always sped up uncontrollably in those moments.
Minutes become seconds and hours inexplicably become microseconds.

It's the theory of relativity

No, if you want to watch time slow to a painful crawl, effectively stopping,
If you want watch everything hang on a balance, on the number of seconds between each breath,
Then you should watch someone die.

It's the Theory of Relativity, like touching your hand to a hot stove,
Seconds become minutes and minutes stretch into infinity.
Plans don't exist in that frozen time,
And therefore time doesn't exist.
Sleep in shifts with your dad so that she's never alone
Type with one hand for work while you hold her hand with your other.
The only time that matters is the alarm that you set faithfully so you don't miss a dose of Ativan
For her
The Ativan is for her,
but by the end you'll wish it was for you.

You'll pray forcefully for the end to come as you realize that the business of dying is painful.
By the end of your shift, you'll sink into sleep just to escape for a few hours the pain of watching it
And to escape the guilt of wanting it to be over
You'll also bargain with God for it to never end because you can't imagine your life without her
Without the smell of her house, a home like no other you have known
Without the softness of her hands
By the end of your shift, you'll sink into sleep just to escape for a few hours the reality that she's leaving you
and the guilt of wanting her to stay, because you know she doesn't want to.
You will laugh with her about the old times when you were both younger
And you will wonder if each thing she says to you will be the last
You will always wonder how she was going to finish the sentence that begin with "I want to warn you, if you should ever get married..."
You will spend some of those hours counting her breaths while she sleeps.
For some reason, it is the metric you have chosen to use to try to guess Death's timing.
It is close. She can see angels.
You can't see anything. You are blind.
You are powerless to Death.
You blink back tears and sing to her.
And for yourself.

She's asleep. And you count her breaths each minute on her wristwatch.
Less than 10 and her breaths are getting shallow.
More than 16, and soon her heart will give out.
You sit there all night..counting. And comparing each count to the last.
Creating averages and medians, charts, and graphs trying to analyze her breaths so that you Can try to wrap your mind around what is happening.
You know what is happening.
She is dying.
And when she breathes her last breath, you will feel confused.
There have been so many breaths that took too long and you stared at her chest willing it to raise
And it did
And now that it has finally come-- that last breath
You wait
Staring at her chest not believing what your eyes are telling you, what your mind is telling you, What your heart is telling you
Every last one of your senses are telling you that she is dead
But still you cannot believe it

Dead.
All of the sudden the world is moving.
It is moving so much faster than it ever did before.
And now not just her breath is gone, but so is her body.
It took a million lifetimes to see that last breath and all of the hope and fear it held,
But in the blink of an eye, you have to re-enter the world at the speed of light
There are people to call
Plans to cancel
Plans to make
An obituary to write
Family to comfort
Wakes to arrange
Flowers to order
A will to execute
Her breath, her body, her house, her things are gone all at once.

But you're still processing that her chest isn't rising.

It's the Theory of Relativity.

Most Days

Most days it's just a vague, unsettled feeling
It's like getting up at 2 am and getting ready for work because I swear I'm late
Waking up in a new place confused about where I am
Wearing my shirt backwards all day and tugging on it, irritated by the strange fit.
Or picking up the phone
And realizing you won't answer.
Most days it goes on like this
Slight bewilderment, discomfort, a dash of disbelief
The hours turn into days and the days into months
And now it would be your birthday
In two more it will be mine
And I can't help but dread the day I know I won't get your birthday card.
Most days it is like this.
But some days, some days it is hard.
A bad day or a stray comment that reminds me of you
And off I go in the middle of the day on a downhill rollercoaster.
And I can't tell you how much I wish you weren't on the moon.
Most days I just wish I could hear you say,
"Honey, you can do no wrong."
One more time.

Springtime

If I write you a letter in my dream,
Could you read it in yours?
Could we go swinging and dancing and climbing through trees
Look at the clouds and stars between the bright green leaves?
Could we meet in our dreams?

Babylon Eyes

Your sad babylon eyes haunt me.
Is it the weight of the world that rests on your throne?
Or is it the blank vastness of a vanished kingdom?
What is the reason for your sad babylon eyes?
Did she cut your hair?
Did you remove her lovely head?
Is it the guilt or fear I see in your sad babylon eyes?
Is it your rotting portrait hidden away
Not in an attic,
but in your eyes.
In the tilt of your head
And the secrets you keep there
The softness of your voice
The poison of your words
Or is it nothing, nothing at all in your sad babylon eyes?
