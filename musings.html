Ode to 2016

2016 was a hard year for me, for so many others, and for the world. But just because it was hard doesn't mean it wasn't a good year,
nor the worst year, at least for me. In 2016, I said goodbye to my first job, my grandmother, a relationship, my uncle, and my home state.
There were some weeks I went into my one-on-one at work, and just said, "I'm stressed."
Moments where I grabbed a beer from the fridge and made Matt sit with me until I felt good enough to "shake it off."
But in those losses, I found a better job and the best co-workers I have ever had,
I developed new connections with my cousins and family in Raleigh, Richmond, and California,
I found my own corner of the world where I feel safe and happy,
where I have found even more new and amazing friends who love Vibrams five-fingers as much as me, go on snowy adventures,
and always seem to understand where I'm coming from no matter how far I've stretched the metaphor.
Thank you, everyone. You made 2016 one of the most rewarding years, and I'm eternally grateful.
Cheers to my friends and family, old, new, and re-connected. I couldn't have done it without you.
